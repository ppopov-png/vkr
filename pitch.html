Dividend change forecasting for listed dividend-paying companies

Short research pitch for the bachelor thesis: predicting the direction of dividend changes (cut / unchanged / increase) using machine learning and financial, market and ESG data.

Pitch table (THREE–TWO–ONE)

(A) Working title
Forecasting dividend policy changes with machine learning
Evidence from listed dividend-paying companies (equities) with quarterly financial, market and ESG data.
(B) Basic research question Can we predict the direction of next-period dividend changes (cut / unchanged / increase) for listed dividend-paying companies using standard financial statements, market factors and ESG scores, and how much does this information improve over simple historical rules?
(C) Key paper(s)
  • Lintner, J. (1956). “Distribution of Incomes of Corporations Among Dividends, Retained Earnings, and Taxes.” The Journal of Finance. – Классическая частичная модель корректировки дивидендов и сглаживания выплат.
  • Baker, M., & Wurgler, J. (2004). “A Catering Theory of Dividends.” The Journal of Finance. – Теория «угождения» спросу инвесторов на дивидендные бумаги.
  • Benartzi, S., Michaely, R., & Thaler, R. (1997). “Do Changes in Dividends Signal the Future or the Past?” The Journal of Finance. – Тест информационного содержания изменений дивидендов.
  • Nissim, D., & Ziv, A. (2001). “Dividend Changes and Future Profitability.” The Journal of Finance. – Связь изменений дивидендов и будущей прибыльности.
  • Yaseen, H., & Dragotă, V. (2021). “Forecasting the Dividend Policy Using Machine Learning Approach: Decision Tree Regression Models.” In Eurasian Business and Economics Perspectives. – Применение ML (decision trees) для прогноза payout ratio.
  • Wang, X. (2024). “Dividend Forecasts via Machine Learning.” – ML-подход к прогнозированию дивидендных выплат и сравнению с аналитиками.
(D) Motivation / Puzzle Dividend cuts and increases are strong signals for investors. Classical models focus on the dividend level or payout ratio, but not on the direction of changes in the near future. At the same time, ML tools are widely used for credit risk and bankruptcy prediction, yet almost never for multi-class dividend change forecasts. The puzzle: can modern ML models extract a stable signal about future dividend actions from standard accounting, market and ESG data?
(E) Idea Build a quarterly panel for listed dividend-paying companies and label three classes of dividend change (–1 cut, 0 unchanged, +1 increase). Compare two model specifications: (i) concurrent quarter-on-quarter changes in fundamentals and market variables; (ii) lagged quarter-on-quarter changes (previous period), used as early signals. Train ML models (logit, Random Forest, CatBoost, XGBoost) and evaluate out-of-sample ROC / 3D ROC to see whether lagged dynamics carry more predictive power than purely contemporaneous changes.
(F) Data
  • NASDAQ dividend history (declaration / record / payment dates, DPS, total dividends);
  • Quarterly financials: assets, equity, debt, cash, income, sales, working capital;
  • Market data: prices, quarterly returns and volatility, betas to S&P 500 and UST 10Y;
  • ESG scores (overall and E/S/G sub-scores).
Time span: about 10–15 years, quarterly frequency, unbalanced panel.
(G) Tools
  • Python (pandas, scikit-learn, XGBoost, CatBoost, Plotly);
  • Multi-class ROC and 3D ROC surfaces for model comparison;
  • Drop-one and permutation feature importance to interpret drivers of the signal;
  • Basic statistical tests and robustness checks (train / test split, out-of-time validation).
(H) What’s new?
  • A modular ML framework that first focuses on direction of dividend changes (cut / unchanged / increase) and is designed to be extended to dividend amount and payout ratios in further work.
  • Systematic comparison of two economic specifications: concurrent quarter-on-quarter changes vs lagged quarter-on-quarter changes in fundamentals, market factors and ESG.
  • Use of modern ML models (gradient boosting, CatBoost / XGBoost) and multi-class ROC + 3D ROC surfaces to visualise model quality for all three classes at once.
  • Detailed feature-level interpretation (drop-one / permutation importance) for dividends, leverage, liquidity, profitability, market risk and ESG, linked directly to economic and managerial narratives.
(I) So what?
  • For investors: earlier warning signals about dividend cuts and more confidence in dividend-growth strategies;
  • For valuation: better inputs for DCF / FCFE models and scenario analysis of payout policies;
  • For managers: feedback on how leverage, liquidity and governance affect the credibility of stable dividend policies;
  • For academia: example of how ML tools can be integrated into classical dividend-policy research.
(J) Contribution
  • A transparent, reproducible ML pipeline for forecasting the direction of dividend changes based on standard financial, market and ESG data for listed dividend-paying companies.
  • Empirical evidence on how much lagged quarter-on-quarter changes in fundamentals and market factors improve prediction quality compared to purely concurrent changes (higher macro AUC and lower classification error).
  • Feature-level insights on the roles of past dividends, leverage, liquidity, profitability, market risk and ESG in driving dividend cuts vs increases, illustrated through importance measures and partial interpretations.
  • An interactive, web-based report (ROC curves, 3D ROC surfaces, diagnostics) that can be reused and extended for future modules of the project, including forecasts of dividend levels and payout ratios.
(K) Other considerations
  • Target outlet: bachelor thesis at HSE; later, potential working paper or blog post on ML in dividend policy;
  • Risks: data quality, survivorship bias, sector heterogeneity; need for careful cleaning and robustness checks;
  • Scope: focus on NASDAQ; extensions can add other exchanges and macro controls;
  • Deliverables: dataset description, code on GitHub, interactive HTML report with all charts.
